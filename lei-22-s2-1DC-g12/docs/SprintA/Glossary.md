# Glossary

**Terms, Expressions and Acronyms (TEA) must be organized alphabetically.**


| **_TEA_** (EN)  | **_TEA_** (PT) | **_Description_** (EN)                                           |                                       
|:------------------------|:-----------------|:--------------------------------------------|
| **Administrator** | **Administrador** | Professional who exercises the function of management in a public or private company or organization.|
| **AGES** | **Agrupamentos de Centros de Saúde** | Guarantee the provision of primary health care to the population of a certain geographical area and looking for health gains.|
| **ARS** | **Administração Regional de Saúde** | Guarantee the region's population access to health care, adapting the available resources to the needs and fulfill and enforce health policies and programs in its area of intervention.|
| **AstraZeneca** | **AstraZeneca** | British-Swedish multinational pharmaceutical and biotechnology company headquartered in Cambridge, England.|
| **Bruteforce Algorithm** | **Algoritmo de Força Bruta** | straightforward methods of solving a problem that rely on sheer computing power and trying every possibility rather than advanced techniques to improve efficiency.|
| **Benchmark Algorithm** | **Algoritmo de Referência** | Tool to analyze statistical learning algorithms on one or more data sets.|
| **Community Mass Vaccination Center** | **Centro de Vacinação Massiva à Comunidade** | Place adopted to vaccinate of a large amount of the communnity.|
| **Coordinator** | **Coordenador** |  Organizes activities and negotiates with others in order to ensure they work together effectively.|
| **Covid-19** | **Covid-19** | Infectious disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus.|
| **Dengue** | **Dengue** | Tropical infectious disease caused by dengue virus, an arbovirus of the family Flaviviridae.|
| **DGS** | **DGS** | State-funded Portuguese healthcare system.|
| **EU** | **União Europeia** | Economic and political union of 27 independent European members.|
| **FIFO** | **Primeiro a chegar, primeiro a sair** | First in, first out.|
| **Healthcare** | **Assistência Médica** | The activity of providing medical services.|
| **Healthcare Center** | **Centro de Saúde** | Community-based organizations that deliver comprehensive, culturally competent, high-quality primary health care services to the community.|
| **Infectius-disease** | **Doença-Infecciosa** | Any disease caused by a pathogen (such as viruses, bacteria, fungi, and parasites)|
| **Immunization** | **Imunização** | Acquisition of immune protection against an infectious disease.|
| **Moderna** | **Moderna** | Pharmaceutical and biotechnology company based in Massachusett|
| **Nurse** | **Enfermeiro** | Health professional, qualified to care for the sick.|
| **OO Software...** | **OO Software** | A computer programming model that organizes software design around data, or objects, rather than functions and logic.|
| **Pfizer** | **Pfizer** | Multinational pharmaceutical company seeded in New York.|
| **Smallpox** | **Varíola** | Infectious disease caused by one of two strains of smallpox virus - variola major and variola minor.|
| **SMS** | **Mensagem** | Message.|
| **SNS** | **SNS** | State organism that ensures every citizen has the right to health.|
| **SVG** | **SVG** | A XML language that describe two-dimensional drawings and graphics in vector form.|
| **Tetanus** | **Tetano** | Bacterial infection characterized by muscle spasms.|
| **User** | **Utilizador** | Person or organization that use a certain type of service.|
| **Vaccination** | **Vacinação** | Act of vaccinating.|
| **Vaccination Certificate** | **Certificado de Vacinação** | Shows the vaccination status of the person at the date of administration of each dose |
